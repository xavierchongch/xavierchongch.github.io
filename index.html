I'm a tax analyst with a bias for clean data. Most of the problems I've solved at work didn't start as automation problems — they started as consistency problems. Once the inputs are standardized, the automation is almost trivial.

On the provision side, I work the full ASC 740 cycle — current and deferred tax, FIN 48 uncertain tax positions, FIN 18 interim reporting, valuation allowance assessments, return-to-provision true-ups, and the deferred tax roll across a portfolio of legal entities. The output feeds into 10-K and 10-Q tax footnotes, statutory financials, and the broader external reporting cycle.

On compliance, my Canadian work covers T2, T5013, T106, NR4, T5, and T4ANR — for public corporations, Canadian and foreign partnerships, and non-resident withholding situations. I also have US tax exposure on the federal and state side, plus Country-by-Country Reporting (CbCR) and transfer pricing on the global side, which keeps the cross-border picture coherent end-to-end.

On the tooling side, I work day-to-day in OneSource Tax Provision and PwC Tax DataHub, and I build my own utilities around them in Python and Power Query — usually to enforce a naming convention, normalize a data source, or make sure two workpapers agree before they hit review.

The throughline: standardize the process, then trust the data, then let the work scale. A clean foundation makes everything downstream — provision, footnotes, audits, transitions — meaningfully easier.
